April 15: Time Well Spent

When we revise the scripts we stage, one of the usual tasks is to reduce the number of roles. In a major company like the Stratford Festival in Ontario, you want lots of smaller parts for aspiring actors to undertake; it’s a sort of internship leading to greater roles and responsibilities. In community theater, you want everyone’s time to be respected, and carrying a spear, or nodding and smiling, or looking scandalized, aren’t a good use of precious hours between work and family and more of both.
